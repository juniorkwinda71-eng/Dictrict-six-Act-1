Act 1 Scene 2
GOLDMAN'S office. GOLDMAN is seated behind his desk. MARY and CASSIEM enter nervously.
Goldman:

Cassiem:

Note: This scene introduces the tension between artistic ambition and the harsh commercial realities facing coloured artists under apartheid. Goldman is sympathetic but operates within a racially stratified system.

Act 1 Summary
Act 1 establishes the vibrant, multicultural community of District Six under apartheid threat. Henry and Hester represent the working-class heart of the community. Mary and Cassiem's musical dreams collide with Goldman's commercial interests and Sandy's presence. Damaka serves as the prophet, warning of the forced removals to come. The act closes with a personal betrayal mirroring the larger betrayal of the community itself.

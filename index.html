Act One ends with Mary discovering Cassiem and Sandy together at Sophia's Blue Moon Jazz Club. The personal betrayal echoes the larger betrayal of the District Six community at the hands of the apartheid government.
Act 1 Summary
Act 1 establishes the vibrant, multicultural community of District Six. Henry and Hester represent the working-class heart of the community. Mary and Cassiem's musical dreams — sparked by Goldman's interest — collide with the threat of forced removals and Sandy's arrival. Nines and the Sexy Boys embody youthful street energy, while Damaka serves as the prophet warning of the community's coming destruction. The personal and political betrayals are woven together at the curtain.

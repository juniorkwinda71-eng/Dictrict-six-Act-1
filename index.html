Act 1 Scene 1
Curtain up on a busy District Six1 street scene. People are going about their business. HENRY is next to his barrow that is filled with fruit and vegetables.
Henry:
En is aaartappel, ywe2 en tamaa-tie – hey! Tomatoes to fry, potatoes to braai. Tomorrow you cry. You know why? 'Cos you didn't buy.
Hawker:
Avocado pere maakie hare mere!
Lekker patat maakie hare glad!
Lekker raap3 virrie aap vannie Kaap!
Galiema:
Henry, is your bananas ripe?
Henry:
Press my number for fresh cucumber.
Galiema:
How much?
Henry:
Don't let your eyes fool you. Don't let your pocket rule you.
Galiema:
Gee net die blerrie prys!
Henry:
Daai's ten for a bob.
Galiema:
Wat? Pick 'n Pay gives twelve for a bob.
Henry:
So go! Go to Pick 'n Pay. 'Cos here you wanna pick but you don't wanna pay.
1. District Six: a community that was home to a rich mosaic of people of different faiths and races who, because of the Group Areas Act of the Nationalist Government, were forcibly removed in the 1970s from the area bordering the city of Cape Town
2. ywe: onions
3. raap: turnips

Press my number for fresh cucumber. [A playful, evasive answer – come find out for yourself.]

Gee net die blerrie prys!
Just give me the damn price!
Henry:
Daai's ten for a bob.
That's ten for a shilling (a bob).
Galiema:
Wat? Pick 'n Pay gives twelve for a bob.
What? Pick 'n Pay gives twelve for a shilling.
Henry:
So go! Go to Pick 'n Pay. Because here you want to pick [things up] but you don't want to pay.

Act 1 Summary
Act 1 establishes the vibrant, multicultural community of District Six under apartheid threat. Henry and Hester represent the working-class heart of the community. Mary and Cassiem's musical dreams collide with Goldman's commercial interests and Sandy's presence. Damaka serves as the prophet, warning of the forced removals to come. The act closes with a personal betrayal mirroring the larger betrayal of the community itself.
